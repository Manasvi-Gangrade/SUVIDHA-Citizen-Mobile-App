3.2 Brief Project Description: 
SUVIDHA is a touch-based, citizen-facing digital KIOSK system designed to provide unified 
self-service access to essential civic utility services including Electricity, Gas, Water Supply, 
and Municipal Services. 
The solution enables citizens to perform tasks such as bill payments, service requests, 
complaint registration, document upload, real-time status tracking, and receipt generation 
without manual staff assistance. Designed with multilingual support, accessibility features, 
and secure authentication, SUVIDHA aims to reduce counter congestion, improve 
transparency, and enhance citizen experience in urban utility offices, aligning with Smart City 
2.0 and Digital India initiatives. 
SUVIDHA transforms fragmented civic service delivery into a unified, citizen-first digital 
experience.

4. Problem Understanding & Objective 
4.1 Understanding of the Problem Statement 
Urban civic utility offices in India handle high volumes of citizen interactions for services related to 
electricity, gas, water supply, sanitation, and municipal grievances. Traditional counter-based service 
delivery models often result in long queues, manual paperwork, limited transparency, and 
inconsistent service quality. Citizens frequently need to visit offices multiple times to submit 
documents, check application status, or resolve complaints. 
The problem statement focuses on designing a touch-based interactive KIOSK interface that can serve 
as a single, integrated self-service platform for citizens. Such a kiosk must be easy to use for people of 
diverse backgrounds, including senior citizens and persons with disabilities, while ensuring secure 
transactions and data privacy. 
The SUVIDHA KIOSK directly addresses these challenges by digitizing routine civic interactions, 
reducing dependency on staff, improving service turnaround time, and enabling transparent, real-time 
access to civic services. This solution is highly relevant for modern urban governance, where scalability, 
accessibility, and efficiency are critical. 
4.2 Objectives of the Proposed Solution 
The proposed SUVIDHA kiosk solution aims to modernize civic service delivery by introducing a 
unified, citizen-centric digital helpdesk that improves accessibility, transparency, and operational 
efficiency within urban utility offices. The key objectives of the solution are: 
ď‚· To provide a standardized, touch-based kiosk with an intuitive interface for accessing 
multiple civic and utility services from a single platform. 
ď‚· To enable complete self-service for bill payments, service requests, grievance registration, 
document submission, and receipt generation, minimizing manual processes. 
ď‚· To ensure inclusive service access through a multilingual and accessibility-first design for 
senior citizens, first-time users, and persons with disabilities. 
ď‚· To implement secure authentication, data handling, and payment mechanisms compliant 
with government IT and data protection regulations. 
ď‚· To enhance transparency through real-time tracking, automated acknowledgements, and 
service status updates. 
ď‚· To reduce administrative overhead by digitizing workflows, standardizing service processes, 
and enabling centralized system monitoring. 
ď‚· To support multi-department integration using a modular architecture for coordinated service 
delivery. 
ď‚· To ensure scalability and future readiness for multi-location deployment and integration of 
additional civic and smart city services. 
5. Technical Design & Architecture 
5.1 Solution Description 
The proposed SUVIDHA solution is a modular, microservices-based digital KIOSK system designed 
for deployment in civic utility offices and public locations. The kiosk provides a touch-first user 
interface through which citizens can access multiple civic services from a single platform. 
Upon interaction, users select their preferred language and authenticate securely. The system then 
presents a unified dashboard displaying available services across Electricity, Gas, and Municipal 
departments. Each service follows a guided workflow, ensuring ease of use even for first-time or low
literacy users. 
The backend is designed using independent microservices that handle authentication, payments, service 
requests, document management, and notifications. This architecture ensures scalability, reliability, and 
ease of integration with existing government systems. 
An Admin Dashboard allows authorized staff to monitor kiosk usage, service demand, complaint 
resolution timelines, and system health, enabling data-driven decision-making.
